Fandom

Yuri!!! On Ice captured the attention of so many, even some who were completely oblivious of "anime" before. This sensation has inspired fans all over the world, as there are even communities and groups on social media dedicated for YOI related content.

Café On Ice

As the popularity of this television series sky-rocketed, its production company and Animate Café had developed an iconic attraction in Ikebukuro, Japan. It opened its doors to the public for the first time on December 1st, but unfortunately, they closed down on December 22nd after the finale of the show (as its purpose was get the audience hyped up for the Grand Prix Final of the show). When the restaurant was of service, customers would have to line up at 9:30 in the morning as it was a first-come-first-serve basis. They would serve adorable Yuri!!!On Ice themed dishes alongside their colourful themed displays and decorations in the store.

Johnny Weir is here!!!

John Garvin Weir , also known as “Johnny Weir”, is an exceptionel figure skater from the United States. He has been on the ice since the age of 12 and has been competing in many (Grand Prix series, Worlds, Olympics, etc.) competitions in both his junior and senior careers. In addition, he is also openly homosexual.

In former interviews, the star was asked about Yuri!!! On Ice , an anime he watched. He found the characters to be very relatable as he share the same profession as them, as he has been an inspiration for the director of Yuri!!! On Ice.

“I changed coaches and competed in Cup of China and Cup of Russia when I was 23 and trying to reinvigorate my career like Yuri-kun. My junior world championship victory was in Sofia, Bulgaria like Viktor. I even was one of the first skaters who traveled with my own special tissue box starring my favorite cartoon, Cheburashka.”

-Johnny Weir 2016 for interview on “The Geekiary”
